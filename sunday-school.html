Sunday School

Forming our children in the Orthodox faith — helping the youngest members of Saint Elias come to know, love, and follow Christ within the life of His Church.

Home › Ministries › Sunday School

From their earliest years, the children of Saint Elias are not merely taught about the faith — they are welcomed into it, learning to pray, to venerate the icons, and to take their place in the worshipping family of the Church.

Our Sunday School exists to walk beside parents in the most important work they will ever do: raising children who know Christ and are at home in His Church. In a warm and joyful setting, our teachers open the Scriptures, the feasts, and the lives of the saints to young hearts — so that the beauty our children see and hear in the Liturgy takes root and grows into a living faith of their own.

Our program

Sunday School at Saint Elias offers classes for children from about age 3 through high school, grouped by age so that each lesson meets children where they are. Little ones begin with the simplest and most beautiful truths of the faith, while older students are drawn into deeper study, discussion, and questions of their own.

Classes meet on Sundays — during or after the Divine Liturgy, so that learning flows directly from worship (current meeting times to confirm). Rather than a curriculum apart from the Church, our teaching is rooted in the very things the children encounter each week: the Holy Scriptures, the cycle of the feasts, the icons that surround them, and the lives of the saints who show what it means to follow Christ.

Above all, our aim is not simply to pass on information but to help form disciples — children who love God, who know they belong to His Church, and who carry that faith with them into the rest of their week and the rest of their lives.

A Faith to Grow Into

What our children learn

Lessons are shaped by age, but woven from the same threads — the Scriptures, the feasts, the saints, and a life of service.

Scripture & the Feasts

The stories of the Old and New Testaments and the great feasts of the Church year, told so that children can follow along Sunday by Sunday.

Lives of the Saints

The saints as real friends and examples — men, women, and children who loved Christ, so our children see that holiness is truly possible.

Icons & the Sacraments

How we pray with icons and share in the sacraments — Baptism, Confession, and Holy Communion — as the living heart of Orthodox life.

Service & Love of Neighbor

Age-appropriate service projects that turn faith into action — teaching children early that to follow Christ is to love and serve others.

Register your child

New families are warmly welcome to join at any time of year. To enroll a child or to ask about class ages and current meeting times, please contact the parish and we will connect you with our Sunday School director. Demo note: an online registration form will be added here before launch.

For parents

You are your child's first and most important teacher in the faith, and Sunday School is here to support you — never to replace the life of prayer and worship at home. On a typical Sunday, bring your children to worship first; the fruit of Sunday School grows best in soil watered by the Liturgy. Class times and locations are shared each week, and our teachers are always glad to tell you what your children are learning so you can carry the conversation home.

Come as you are, and don't worry if your family is new to Orthodoxy or if your children are still learning to sit and stand along with everyone else — wiggles and questions are entirely at home here. If you have children with particular needs, simply let us know, and we will do our best to help every child feel welcome.

Help as a teacher or aide

Our program depends on parishioners who are willing to share their faith with the next generation. You do not need to be a trained teacher — only a practicing Orthodox Christian with a love for children and a willingness to learn. Teachers and classroom aides serve on a rotation so that no one carries the whole year alone, and lesson materials and guidance are provided.

If you would like to help form our children in the faith, please see our volunteers page or speak with Fr. George or the Sunday School director. It is one of the most rewarding ways to serve the parish.
